"How you live affects how long you live." - Paul Graham

"The days are long but the decades are short." - Sam Altman

I've noticed that a surprising number of things have been deliberately made to waste my time. Things you do that waste your time must be really good at tricking you. Somehow they manage to seem important in the moment, but never after. Will you remember the endless Facebook posts a month from now?

Time-wasters dilute your energy - that's why they're a waste. You end up spreading out your focus so broadly that none of it matters.

Does time matter? I think so.

But they don't always trick you. There are some time-wasters we put up with out of necessity. Hypothesis: how long a day feels is proportional to how many new or funny things happened that day.
